## ICD10

`ICD-10` (International Classification of Diseases, Tenth Revision) is the global system used to code and classify diseases, conditions, and medical procedures. It expands on ICD-9 with far more detail, offering tens of thousands of codes to capture specific diagnoses and scenarios. This precision improves patient records, insurance billing, research, and tracking of public health trends. ICD-10 is critical for ensuring consistency and accuracy across healthcare systems worldwide. It is the current international standard for medical coding.
